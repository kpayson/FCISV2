1. How do we access information on Legacy facilities?
    2.

    Legacy facilities are those who perform activities that are similar to those performed in APFs, but either pre-date the start of the APF program, or were earlier granted this status due to the low risk inherent in the activities performed. There is an initiative being planned to transition the enduring legacy facilities into full APF status, including documentation, oversight and support, that is pending approval and implementation. Non-enduring facilities, being those which have a planned obsolescence and removal from service will continued to be retired as replacement facilities come online, at which point they may be refurbished, repurposed, or in some cases are slated for demolition. As the Legacy transition program proceeds and APF documentation becomes available, pages will be developed and populated for these facilities on this site. In the meantime, contact the FCIS document administrator for assistance with these facilities.

  2. What are the common design failures in APFs?
    3.
    1. Inadequate engineering controls
    2. Inadequate HVAC robustness, redundancy, and controls
    3. Excessive noise and vibration
    4. Failure to adequately account for and coordinate door hardware and appurtenances in plan and elevation
    5. Inadequate hand hygiene, toilet, and locker facilities to allow for sanitary operation
    6. Insufficient size, organization, and ventilation rates of PPE donning/doffing areas
    7. Excessive plumbing fixtures, including sinks and plumbed eyewashes
    8. Inadequate access control
