The Facilities Compliance and Inspection Section (FCIS) is a section of the Technical Support Branch (TSB), of the Division of Technical Resources (DTR), within the Office Of Research Facilities (ORF), Office of the Director (OD), National Institutes of Health (NIH).

FCIS is responsible for providing Quality Assurance (QA) oversight of the design, construction, commissioning, qualification, operations and maintenance of NIH Aseptic Production Facilities (APF) to ensure compliance to regulatory requirements. To this end, FCIS provides a broad range of services, among which include:

  • Review of APF Permit Review Documents (Drawings, Specifications, Basis Of Design)
  • Review of APF Non-Permit Review Documents (Statement Of Requirements, User Requirement Specifications, Validation Master Plan, etc.)
  • Assist Integrated Project Teams in developing projects which conform to GMP and GxP.
  • Develop facility-oriented APF SOPs and provide QA Oversight of the DFOM SOPs.
  • Monitor critical environmental parameters (TEMP, RH, dP, and ACH) at APFs and oversee deviations and other out of specification incidents, including oversight of root cause analysis (RCA), and corrective and preventative action (CAPA)
  • Develops and maintains Chapter-13 of the Design Requirements Manual (DRM) which provides detailed design requirements and guidance specific to APFs.
  • Oversee the DFOM operations and maintenance of APFs, including work plans, critical parameter sensor calibration, facility HEPA certifications, training, etc.
  • Ensure that the NIH APFs are maintained to current facility requirements to reduce the risk of contamination, mix-ups, deviations, failures, and errors to assure that drug products meet quality standards.
  • Hold and manage facility-related critical documents.
