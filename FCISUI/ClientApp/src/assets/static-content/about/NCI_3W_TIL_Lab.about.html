The National Institutes of Health (NIH) National Cancer Institute (NCI) Surgery Branch operates the 3-West facility for the manufacture of Phase I and II clinical materials to support the development of autologous Tumor Infiltrating Leukocyte (TIL) cell therapies. The facility complies with current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP). The space is approximately 796 gross square feet and is located on the 3rd floor of the southwest wing of the Clinical Research Center in Building 10.

The facility consists of one (1) ISO 7 positively pressurized processing room and one (1) ISO 8 gowning/de-gowning anteroom. An ISO 5 environment for work is maintained in the Biological Safety Cabinets.

The processing room is designed to provide a multi-directional flow of personnel, equipment, and materials.

The mechanical systems include two (2) air handling units and approximately three (3) terminal air units that will supply and control air changes, temperature, humidity and differential pressure. All supply air to the classified rooms is HEPA filtered. The exhaust system ties to the laboratory exhaust system.
