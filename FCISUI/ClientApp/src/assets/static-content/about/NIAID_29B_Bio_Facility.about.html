Clinical Center, Pilot Bioproduction Facility (PBF)


The National Institutes of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID) will operate the Pilot Bioproduction Facility in room B1G09l located in the basement of Building 29B at the NIH Bethesda site. The Pilot Plant is approximately 720 square feet. The facility is designed to support campaigned process operations that are used to generate clinical scale Bulk Drug Substance (BDS). These operations include fermentation, purification, and subsequent conjugation of recombinant malarial protein and carrier protein. The generated BDS is planned for use in phase 1 and 2 vaccine trials and therefore, the Process Suite is also treated as a classified production facility in compliance with cGMPs. The facility is currently in design with anticipated turnover by December of 2021.

Open manipulations are carried out within a Class II, Type A2 BSC located in an ISO 8 Process Suite background. Closed processing is carried out in equipment located adjacent to the biosafety cabinet.

New partitions in classified and graded spaces shall be Modular Cleanroom Walls. Existing partitions shall contain Cleanroom Liner Panel. New ceilings shall be gasketed, load bearing (walkable) A red/green indicator lamps will be provided.

Stainless steel handwash sinks and sterile emergency eyewash stations and recessed safety shower station shall be provided. New lighting fixtures in all spaces except for Equipment Room B1G11 shall be recessed fully gasketed cleanroom LEDâ€™s

A new recirculating air handling unit will serve the Process Suite. The AHU will be suspended above the new walkable ceiling and will be accessible from a ladder located in the adjacent equipment room.

The air inside the cleanroom will require HEPA-filtration, differential air pressure and temperature and humidity control

BAS System will be provided for the Process Suite operations and will control and monitor the space environmental parameters (Temp, %RH, dP). A new dedicated Equipment and Environmental Monitoring System (EMS) shall be provided to monitor the space environmental parameters (Temp, %RH, dP), Equipment Alarms and Critical Utilities Monitoring as identified by the owner The EMS will be a qualified system.
