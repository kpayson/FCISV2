The National Institutes of Health (NIH) Center for Cellular Engineering (CCE) operates the 3T facility for the manufacture of Phase I and II clinical materials to support the development of autologous and allogeneic cell therapies. The facility complies with current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP). The space is approximately 3,257 gross square feet with additional support and mechanical spaces; it is located on the 3rd floor of the T wing in Building 10.

The facility consists of five (5) ISO 7 positively pressurized Tissue Culture rooms, an ISO 8 equipment storage room, a CNC storage freezer room, gowning/de-gowning areas, a supply room, a utility room, and additional laboratory and support areas. An ISO 5 environment for tissue work is maintained in the Biological Safety Cabinets.

Tissue Culture rooms are designed to provide a multi-directional flow of personnel, equipment, and materials.

The mechanical systems include two (2) air handling units, a recirculating air handler, and approximately twelve (12) terminal air units that will supply and control air changes, temperature, humidity, and differential pressure. All supply air to the classified rooms is HEPA filtered. There is a dedicated exhaust system with redundant exhaust fans.
