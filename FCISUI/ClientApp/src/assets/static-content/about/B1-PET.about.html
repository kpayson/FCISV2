The National Institutes of Health (NIH) Clinical Center (CC) Positron Emission Tomography (PET) Department operates the PET facility for the release of research PET doses for use in the NIH Clinical Center. The facility is regulated by US Pharmacopeia General Chapter <823> Radiopharmaceuticals for Positron Emission Tomography, and its dispensing activities are governed by other USP General Chapters. The space is approximately 367 gross square feet and is located on the first floor of Building 10.

The facility consists of a non-classified, negatively pressurized segregated compounding room which houses three Primary Engineering Control (PEC) cabinets. An ISO 5 environment is maintained within the Biological Safety Cabinet, Fume Hood, and Laminar Flow Hood.

The room is designed to provide a multi-directional flow of personnel, equipment, and materials.

The mechanical system includes a base building air handling unit and manifolded hazardous redundant exhaust fans. Control is maintained by terminal exhaust boxes for the room and a dedicated exhaust box for each of the ducted PECs.
